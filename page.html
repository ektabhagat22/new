A 14-year-old app developer, Ekta Bhagat has created AI and AR based App called SDDS

Posted By: sumant August 02,2022

how app works this mobile app is specially designed for down syndrome kids,which allows a user to read alphabets/numbers of varying sizes on the screen of their handsets. Based on their level of reading accuracy, the app will provide the users with a vision score. this app has advance AR features engage language learners with an animated chatbot as well as visualizations of objects and words to help make learning more dynamic, as well as providing instant feedback on pronunciation and any errors.

These AR features come in addition to SDDS lessons, games, and practice conversations designed to cover more than 30 different languages.Ekta set about researching, developing and testing her own version of a diagnosis program. this program undergoes testing at two research centres.

Ekta Bhgat has also won one of the nineth place in a competition dubbed as Silicon Valley Programs which was conducted by the institute for the students. Sood was one of the 70,000 kids across the world who had participated in the competition, where applicants were required to showcase their out-of-the-world creations that would solve a real-life issue.

“Kids today are very aware of societal issues and want to contribute in their own way to address them. We are witnessing many young kids like Ekta creating truly transformational and high impact that will make a lasting impact on the world.”, said Karan Bajaj, Founder and CEO, WhiteHat Jr. 

Ekta said"More to go! I am so glad that my results of hardwork and sleepless nights are finally here.She also said that"right now i am working on my web3.0 project that will boom this tech industry i'm pretty sure about it! stay tuned for that.

As part of the program, Ekta Bhagat will also be given a chance to pitch the SDDS app to venture capitalists in Silicon Valley, San Francisco, USA. in this program, she will visit the Silicon Valley in San Francisco, California and describe it to the top venture capitalists (VCs) like Nexus Venture Partners and Owl Ventures in upcoming silicon valley project 2022. she will also learn about entrepreneurship, get an opportunity to visit Googleplex and interact with their engineers, as well as visit the Waymo facility
